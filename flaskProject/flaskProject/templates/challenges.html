Challenges

During the development process, we encountered several challenges that shaped our approach and outcomes. Initially, finding a suitable dataset aligned with our project objectives posed a significant challenge. Many publicly available datasets lacked the necessary features and targets required for our breast cancer prediction system. However, after careful consideration, we identified a dataset that met our requirements and proceeded with our development efforts.

Another challenge we faced was selecting the most effective machine learning algorithm for breast cancer tumor stage prediction. We trained multiple algorithms and evaluated their performance. Decision trees exhibited overfitting issues, while AdaBoost yielded moderate results. However, both random forest and gradient boosting classifier showed promising outcomes. After careful consideration, we chose gradient boosting due to its interpretability, reduced bias, and ability to handle imbalanced data. These advantages contributed to improved predictive power and made gradient boosting the ideal choice for accurate predictions and fair treatment of minority classes.

Lessons learned from this project were valuable in terms of managing expectations. We realized the importance of aligning expectations with available resources and technology. Initially, we had ambitious goals, but we encountered limitations related to dataset availability and technological constraints. Moving forward, we will conduct thorough research and assessments to better understand the feasibility and compatibility of datasets and technology. By managing expectations effectively, we can set realistic goals and deliverables, ultimately improving project success.
